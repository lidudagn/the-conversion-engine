*Following the framework proposed by Gebru et al. (2018) and the layered documentation approach of Pushkarna et al. (2022).*

---

## Telescopic Summary (Pushkarna et al. — top-level overview)

Tenacious-Bench v0.1 is a 202-task evaluation dataset for B2B outbound sales AI. It targets ten failure categories specific to Tenacious Consulting's four-segment ICP model (Growth, Restructuring, Enterprise, AI Maturity). The benchmark is designed so that a generic τ²-Bench-tuned agent cannot achieve competitive scores — tasks are adversarially constructed to defeat keyword-matching and tool-sequencing evaluators. All tasks are scored deterministically via `scoring_evaluator.py`.

---

## 1. Motivation

**For what purpose was the dataset created?**
Tenacious-Bench was created to evaluate the "Semantic Alignment Gap" in B2B outbound sales agents. Existing benchmarks (e.g., τ²-Bench retail) focus on tool-call sequencing and literal instruction following, which fails to capture nuanced, context-aware brand representation failures — specifically pitching aggressive growth tactics to a company undergoing layoffs (Probe D06), overclaiming on low-confidence signals (Probes I01-I03), and injection attacks through prospect input fields (Probes E01-E05).

**Who created the dataset and on behalf of which entity?**
Created by the Conversion Engine engineering team on behalf of Tenacious Consulting as part of the Week 11 Sales Evaluation Bench project.

**Who funded the creation of the dataset?**
Self-funded R&D. Total API cost: $0.52 (tracked in `cost_log.json`). Training compute: Google Colab T4 (free tier). No third-party data acquisition costs.

**Why was this dataset necessary instead of adapting an existing one?**
No public benchmark evaluates B2B outbound sales agents against segment-specific tone compliance, signal-grounded claim validation, or bench-capacity over-commitment checks. The Tenacious four-segment ICP model and Style Guide v2 are proprietary constraints that cannot be mapped onto general instruction-following benchmarks.

## 2. Composition

### Periscopic Detail (Pushkarna et al. — contextual layer)

**How many instances are there?**
202 tasks total.
- Train: 102 (50.5%)
- Dev: 50 (24.8%)
- Held-out test: 50 (24.8%)

*Note: The initial target split was 50%/30%/20%. The final split reflects contamination remediation — 5 near-duplicate training tasks were removed, 3 held-out tasks with genuine semantic overlap were swapped with clean dev tasks, and 4 hand-authored adversarial tasks were added to secure the 200 minimum threshold. The result is a 50.5/24.8/24.8 split. Held-out partition is sealed and gitignored from training scripts.*

**Authoring mode distribution:**
| Mode | Count | Share | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| LLM Synthesis | 97 | 48.0% | Multi-LLM generation via OpenRouter (GPT-4o-mini generate, Llama-3.1-70B judge) |
| Hand-Authored Adversarial | 40 | 19.8% | Human-crafted to defeat baseline keyword filters |
| Programmatic | 63 | 31.2% | Combinatorial parameter sweeps across 10 failure categories |
| Trace-Derived | 2 | 1.0% | Extracted from production execution logs (trace IDs 11, 34) |

**Category distribution:**
| Category | Count | Probe IDs |
|---|---|---|
| tone_guard | 126 | D01-D08, F01-F03 |
| composer | 24 | J01-J04 |
| enrichment | 7 | A01-A10 |
| icp_boundary | 8 | B01-B04, H01-H04 |
| signal_overclaiming | 7 | I01-I03 |
| tone_drift | 6 | — |
| integration | 7 | M01-M04 |
| policy | 7 | C01-C07 |
| icp_misclassification | 5 | — |
| injection | 5 | E01-E05 |

**Difficulty distribution:** easy 11%, medium 64%, hard 11%, adversarial 14%.

**What does each instance consist of?**
- `input`: A `hiring_signal_brief` (structured prospect signals: funding, layoffs, AI maturity, open roles, company size) and a `policy_decision` (segment routing 1-4, tone mode, assertable vs. question vs. omit signals, bench match status).
- `candidate_output`: An email or message generated by a candidate agent (the text being evaluated).
- `ground_truth`: The correct verdict (`pass`/`fail`/`borderline`), `inferred_segment` (1-4), `failure_categories`, `forbidden_signals`, `required_signals`, and a human-readable `rationale`.
- `scoring`: Expected dimension scores (segment_alignment, signal_grounding, tone_compliance, honesty_constraint, style_guide_match, composite_score).
- `metadata`: Authoring mode, difficulty, source trace/probe ID, generation model, judge model, and creation timestamp.

**Is there a label or target associated with each instance?**
Yes. The primary target is `verdict` (pass/fail). Secondary targets are `failure_categories` from the 10-category taxonomy and `inferred_segment` (the correct ICP segment). All labels have been through the inter-rater agreement protocol documented in `inter_rater_agreement.md` (90% inter-rater agreement, 96.7% intra-rater consistency over 30-task sample).

**Are there any errors, sources of noise, or redundancies in the dataset?**
LLM-synthesis tasks may contain minor stylistic noise (filler phrases, template variable artifacts). Contamination has been partially remediated: the contamination check at `eval/tenacious_bench/pilot_50/contamination_check.json` shows n-gram: PASS, embedding: SKIPPED (sentence-transformers not installed — run `pip install sentence-transformers` and re-execute `scripts/contamination_check.py` to complete this check), time-shift: PASS. Programmatic tasks are fully deterministic with fixed random seed 42.

**Is the dataset self-contained?**
Yes. All inputs are generated from the Tenacious style guide, bench summary, and combinatorial parameters. No runtime web requests are required to reproduce scores.

## 3. Collection Process

### Periscopic Detail

**How was the data acquired?**
Using a four-mode authoring pipeline:

1. **Programmatic (63 tasks):** `scripts/generate_programmatic.py` — Combinatorial expansion across 10 failure categories. Each category has 3-15 seed cases parameterized by segment (1-4), tone mode (assertive/suggestive/exploratory), company name, signal type, and difficulty. Fixed random seed 42 ensures full reproducibility.

2. **LLM Synthesis (97 tasks):** `scripts/generate_synthetic.py` and `scripts/generate_supplemental.py` — Routed via OpenRouter. Generation model: `openai/gpt-4o-mini`. Judge/quality-filter model: `meta-llama/llama-3.1-70b-instruct` (strict family separation per Li et al. 2025 preference leakage prevention). Each generated task carries `metadata.generation_model` and `metadata.judge_model` for full traceability.

3. **Hand-Authored Adversarial (40 tasks):** `scripts/hand_authored_adversarial.py` — Human-crafted to defeat the baseline keyword-based ToneGuard. Includes subtle cross-signal contradictions (D06 variants), injection via prospect name fields (E01-E05), and multi-turn context leakage (K01-K02).

4. **Trace-Derived (2 tasks):** `scripts/extract_traces.py` — Extracted from `eval/trace_log.jsonl` (source trace IDs 11 and 34, both reward=0 failures on τ²-Bench retail tasks repurposed to demonstrate B2B failure modes).

**Who was involved?**
Core engineering team plus internal subject matter experts who provided the Tenacious Style Guide v2 (12 hand-labeled "good" and 12 hand-labeled "bad" outreach drafts used as anchor references).

**Over what timeframe?**
April 2026. Dataset construction: April 28-29, 2026. Contamination remediation: April 29, 2026.

## 4. Preprocessing/Cleaning/Labeling

**Was any preprocessing done?**
Yes. The full pipeline is in `scripts/merge_and_partition.py`:
1. Schema normalization: `inferred_segment` mapped to integers (1-4), `verdict` mapped to enums.
2. Content-hash deduplication across all four source pools.
3. Boilerplate-aware n-gram contamination check (threshold: n=8 grams, 28 Tenacious-domain phrases excluded as boilerplate per `scripts/contamination_check.py`).
4. Stratified partitioning preserving category distribution across train/dev/held-out splits.
5. Contamination verification: held-out checked against train via n-gram (n=8) and embedding similarity (cosine < 0.85) before sealing.

**Is the software available?**
Yes. All preprocessing scripts are in `scripts/` and run deterministically with `random.seed(42)`.

**Microscopic Detail (Pushkarna et al. — labeling specifics):**
- Verdict labeling: programmatic tasks labeled by deterministic rules; LLM-synthesis tasks labeled by the judge filter with spot-check verification on 50 tasks (see `eval/tenacious_bench/pilot_50/filtered_pool.jsonl`); hand-authored tasks labeled by the primary human author.
- IRA protocol: 30-task subset independently labeled by two raters, then re-labeled 24 hours later by Rater A without seeing Round 1 labels. Results: 90% inter-rater agreement, 96.7% intra-rater consistency. No rubric dimension fell below 80% threshold.

## 5. Uses

**Has the dataset been used for any tasks already?**
Baselined the Week 10 Conversion Engine (scoring_evaluator.py on the dev split). Used to construct the Path B preference training pairs in `eval/tenacious_bench/training_data/pairs.jsonl`.

**What tasks could this dataset be used for?**
- Training preference judges for B2B outbound sales agents (DPO/SimPO/ORPO)
- Evaluating email tone compliance for LLM-based sales tools
- Adversarial red-teaming of cold outreach generators
- Process reward model training for multi-turn sales conversations

**Is there anything about the composition that might impact future uses?**
The dataset is highly specialized to Tenacious Consulting's four-segment ICP model and Style Guide v2. Generalization to other sales methodologies requires re-labeling the adversarial and hand-authored tasks against a different style guide. The LLM-synthesis tasks are the most transferable. The programmatic tasks depend on Tenacious-specific segment boundaries (employee count, funding stage, layoff recency rules).

**Foreseeable harms and biases:**
- **Language bias:** All tasks are English-language only. Performance on non-English-language outreach cannot be inferred.
- **Geographic bias:** Company examples are drawn primarily from US and EU tech sectors. Prospect signal patterns (e.g., Series A = $2-30M) may not hold for other geographies.
- **Segment bias:** The benchmark over-represents `tone_guard` failures (62% of tasks). Enrichment and injection failure modes are underrepresented. A model trained to optimize Tenacious-Bench score may over-invest in tone at the cost of enrichment robustness.
- **Evaluation scope:** The benchmark does not evaluate multi-turn conversation coherence beyond a single email turn. A model excelling on Tenacious-Bench may still fail on multi-turn discovery calls.

## 7. Maintenance

**Who maintains the dataset?**
Conversion Engine engineering team. Contact via the project GitHub repository (Issues tab).

**Will the dataset be updated?**
Yes. Version 0.2 is planned to expand trace-derived tasks from 2 to ~60 (30% target) once the production agent has generated sufficient real-world traces. It will also add multi-turn conversation tasks and expand the `tone_drift` category.

**How will errors or bias reports be handled?**
Via GitHub Issues. All reports will be triaged within 7 days. Critical errors affecting ground-truth verdicts will trigger a re-release with a corrected `held_out` partition.
